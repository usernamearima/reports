---

# 1. Overview
The room introduces defensive security fundamentals, focusing on strategies and tools used to **detect, investigate, and respond to cyber threats**. It contrasts defensive approaches with offensive techniques and provides practical experience via a SIEM simulation.

---

# 2. Key Concepts Covered
The following defensive security domains were explored:

- **Defensive Security Definition:** Focuses on **detecting and responding to attacks** rather than exploiting systems.
- **Blue Team Overview:** Defensive security teams are commonly known as the **Blue Team**. 
- **Security Operations Centre (SOC):** A group monitoring networks and systems for malicious events. 
- **Threat Intelligence:** Gathering, analysing, and applying knowledge about adversaries to strengthen defences.  
- **Digital Forensics and Incident Response (DFIR):** Techniques for preserving and analysing evidence and responding to incidents. 
- **Malware Analysis:** Understanding malware behaviour through static and dynamic analysis. 

---

# 3. Practical SIEM Exercise
A simulated **Security Information and Event Management (SIEM)** dashboard was used to provide hands‑on defensive experience:

- Review alerts generated by the SIEM to identify suspicious traffic. 
- Investigate events such as unauthorized access attempts shown in the dashboard. 
- Respond by blocking malicious IPs and applying rate limiting or security rule changes.

Successful containment and mitigation in the simulation produced the room’s practical flag.

---

# 4. Flag Discovery
The practical exercise culminated in a flag obtained after investigating and stopping suspicious activity in the SIEM simulation:

**Flag obtained:** `THM{THREAT-BLOCKED}`

---

# 5. Defensive Techniques Demonstrated
The room reinforced several defensive best practices:

- **Monitoring System Activity:** Continuous review of logs and alerts to identify anomalies. 
- **Incident Classification:** Determining whether alerts represent real threats. 
- **Threat Containment:** Blocking malicious actors and preventing further compromise.   
- **Response Strategy Implementation:** Using tools and rules to reduce attack success. 

---

# 6. Impact
An effective defensive security approach ensures:

- Early detection of intrusion attempts. 
- Reduced exposure to attacks through proactive investigation.  
- Improved incident handling via established response processes.

These skills are vital in real‑world SOC environments.

---

# 7. Key Takeaways
- Defensive security is centred on **detecting and responding to threats**, not exploiting systems.  
- **SOC analysts** play a crucial role in monitoring systems and investigating alerts.  
- SIEM tools are foundational in aggregating, analysing, and responding to security data. 
- Hands‑on experience with alerts and block actions enhances understanding of real‑world defence operations. 

---

# SUMMARY
The **Intro to Defensive Security** room provides a foundational view of blue team operations on TryHackMe. It covers:

- Defensive security principles and comparisons with offensive approaches.  
- Roles and responsibilities within a **Security Operations Centre**. 
- Practical SIEM investigation and response leading to the flag.

Flow: Concept Introduction → Defensive Domains → SIEM Simulation → Alert Investigation → Response → Flag Retrieval
